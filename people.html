Georgetown Forest STEAM Educators

Ms. Aina is the Founder of the Georgetown Forest STEAM School and a longtime Mathematics Educator. She's has been instructing various subjects ranging from Multi-sensory Early, Elementary and Middle School Mathematics, Geometry, Science to Theatrical Linguistics, Poem Reading, Storytelling and Acting, Miming and Movements, Theatrical Speaking/Communication, and Puppetry. Aina holds a Master's degree in Sustainable Development with a focus in Education in conflict prone zones from the Brandeis University, Massachusetts. Her thesis's core message is bringing holistic education practices in conflict prone settings can prevent potential conflicts. She successfully completed her Certificate Program in Entrepreneurship in Emerging Economies by the Harvard School of Business.
She blends her STEAM Curriculum with Nature-based practices and Play-based techniques. She believes in being harmonious with Nature is vital and children should spend their childhood learning from Nature. Speaking of a play-based element of learning, she's a strong believer that the concept of Theatrical Linguistics is a powerful instrument to groom future leaders, scientists, musicians, poets, dancers and just loving personalities. She has completed a certificate course "Writing Successfully for the Stage" by the University of Cambridge and a certificate program "Theatre for Kids". She resides in Washington, D.C. with her daughter.

Advisory Board

Hatice Sahinoglu is a longtime educator, a brilliant mathematician. She holds her Master's degree from the London School of Economics and received her PhD degree in Mathematics from Brown University. She's a strong believer that female students are great mathematicians, and the role of education in one's life is vital. She's married, her husband and Dr. Sahinoglu have a son and reside in Silver Spring, MD. She gardens and a deep nature lover.

Sila Ataca is a biomedical researcher. She holds a PhD degree in Microbiology from Boston University and Master's degree in Biotechnology Management from the IE Business School in Spain. Having educated herself in child development after having a child, she strongly believes in the benefits of playing and learning outdoors. Nature-based educational programs support the social, emotional, behavioral and intellectual development of children and need to be widely available (which is sadly not the case). She resides in Bethesda, MD. Among her favorite activities are camping, reading and spending time with loved ones.
